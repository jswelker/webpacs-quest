General Information

What is Quest?

Quest is a group of Missouri libraries within the MOBIUS library consortium (see below). The Quest Library Catalog is an on-line public access catalog, including the collections of the following Missouri libraries: Central Methodist University, University of Central Missouri, Missouri Valley College, and State Fair Community College.

[back to top]

What is MOBIUS?

MOBIUS is a consortium of libraries in the state of Missouri. The membership includes 68 full members from colleges, universities, the Missouri State Library, and 2 public libraries (cooperating partners). [more]

[back to top]

How long will I be able to keep books that I borrow?

Each library within Quest has its own limits regarding the time one can keep materials.

Most materials borrowed from other libraries may be kept for 21 days. You may renew each item twice for 21 days at each renewal if the item is not needed elsewhere. When allowed, renewal due dates are calculated at 21 days from the time the item is actually renewed and not 21 days from the old due date. Be certain to read all confirmation messages after each renewal attempt.

[back to top]

How many books can I have out at one time?

Each library within Quest has its own limits regarding total items checked out.

MOBIUS restricts the number of items that can be checked out at one time to 10 items for undergraduates and 20 items for faculty and graduate students.

Help with Searching

KEYWORD Searching

Keyword searching looks for the terms that you enter anywhere in the title, journal title, author, subject, series, notes, organization name, corporate name (including publishers), and many book chapter titles. Use keyword searching when you are unsure of the exact title, author, or LC Subject Heading.

Words entered next to each other are treated as a phrase, so “Winnie the Pooh” will find this character string. To search two or more words that are not next to each other, you must type and between each word. For more information, see the search tips under keyword search.

Use Boolean operators ( and , or , and and not ) to refine your search. You may also use parentheses to nest them:

Examples:

KEYWORD: plants AND diseases
KEYWORD: beer OR ale
KEYWORD: mexico AND NOT new
KEYWORD: (ozarks OR mountains) AND missouri
KEYWORD: (cats OR dogs OR pets) AND NOT laboratory

Use the asterisk * to truncate your search terms; use double asterisks ** for more than 6 characters:

Example: KEYWORD: comput** retrieves computer, computers, computing, computation, etc.

[back to top]


TITLE Searching

Title searching retrieves titles of books, journals, and newspapers (including series and chapter titles when available.) The catalog automatically truncates your title search:

Example: TITLE: Engineering will retrieve Engineering, Engineering and Design, Engineering Economist, etc.

If you do not want the catalog to truncate your title, use the | bar symbol:
Example: TITLE: Superstar| will retrieve Superstar but not Superstar Team.

Search tip: Normally, initial articles, such as “a,” “an,” or “the,” are ignored in title searches. If you do wish to retrieve titles beginning with any of these words (e.g., the title "A to Z : Alphabet Picture Book"), type the initial article twice, separated by a space. For example, entering "A A TO Z Alphabet Picture Book" will search for the desired title. Use this method to search for titles beginning with initial articles in any language. For an extensive list of articles in many languages, see the section "What are initial articles?”

[back to top]


AUTHOR Searching

Author searching retrieves any type of author (individuals, organizations, composers, artists, editors, etc.). Enter last name first for personal names:

Example: AUTHOR: Bronte Emily
Example: AUTHOR: American Institute of Physics

The catalog automatically truncates the name that you enter:

Example: AUTHOR: Cavan retrieves Cavan, Cavana, Cavanaugh, etc.

JOURNAL, MAGAZINE, AND NEWSPAPER TITLE Searching

You can find journals, magazines, and newspapers by doing a regular title search. If you want to limit your search to only these material types, use the journal title search option. You may need to know the city of publication for common titles:

Example: Time is listed as Time Chicago, IL

Titles of individual articles are not listed in the catalog. Use your library's indexes and electronic databases to find articles.

[back to top]


SUBJECT Searching

Subject searching retrieves official Library of Congress Subject Headings, and these can be tricky. (For example, the LC Subject Heading for the "French Revolution" is "France – History -- Revolution,1789-1799. ")

If you are unsure of the correct subject heading for your topic, try doing a keyword search instead. Use the records you retrieve to identify the correct LC Subject Headings, and then do a new subject search.

I got too many items with my search.

Here are some ways that you can decrease the number of items you retrieve:

  1. Do a Subject search instead of a keyword search. LC Subject searches cover a standardized list of terms and do not search other fields, such as author, note, etc. Children's subject searches can be used to search for children's materials on a particular topic.
  2. Use the word AND to do a keyword search on more than one concept. The computer will look for items that mention both terms.

           television AND children
  3. Use the words AND NOT to further limit your search. The computer will look for items that mention the first term but omit any items that mention the term after the AND NOT.

           South America AND NOT Brazil
  4. When viewing a record for an item you retrieved, check the "Subjects" field to find more specific headings/terms. Scroll down through the full Quest record for an item you retrieved until you see the LC Subject field. Here you will find subject terms you may use.
  5. Use the Limit/Sort button to narrow to a particular publication year or years, material type, language, etc. When this action is performed, the search results will be limited accordingly.

    6. I got too few items with my search.

    Here are some ways that you can increase the number of items you retrieve:

    1. Do a Keyword search instead of a LC Subject or Children's Subject search. A keyword search covers more fields, such as title, notes, etc.

    2. Use the word OR to search on variations of a concept. To give the computer more choices, use OR to link two words with similar meaning ( mathematics OR arithmetic). You may also use OR to link two words, either of which would satisfy your needs (Greek OR Latin).

    3. Use an asterisk* to truncate endings of words. Have the computer search for variations of a word by adding an asterisk. For example, to search for training or trainers or train, type:

            train*

    I didn't get anything!

    Here are some suggestions if your search gets zero results:

    1. Check your spelling. Unfortunately, the computer won't do this for you. You can use an asterisk * if you know at least part of a word.

            Dostoevsk* (will find Dostoevsky or Dostoevskii)

      If you want the computer to fill in more than 5 letters, use two asterisks:

            totalit** (for a search on totalitarianism)

    2. Do a keyword search. This is the most general type of search you can do.

    3. Make sure you are doing the correct type of search. Trying to search for an author in the journal name field usually won't work. Check that you have selected the correct type of search.

    4. Do a simple search on fewer keywords. Try simplifying your search by limiting the number of words you are stringing together with the word And. For example, instead of personnel and training and computer-assisted instruction and evaluation, try the following:

            computer-assisted instruction and evaluation
            training and computer-assisted instruction

    5. Try the search in the entire MOBIUS catalog to see if another library/cluster has the materials you want. It's possible that your library just doesn't own the materials you need. Follow the link at the top of the screen to search the MOBIUS catalog.

    I want to limit my search but am confused by the "Material Type" options.

    Most of the material types are familiar ones, such as books, journals, maps, etc. Some of the other ones are:

    Thesis or Manuscript: Thesis includes master's theses and doctoral dissertations. Manuscripts may include authored works, correspondence, and other documents related to an individual or group. Manuscripts are often kept in separate collections.

    Electronic Resource: Resources such as online databases, cd-roms, etc. As a rule, regular music cds are grouped under Music Recordings rather than Electronic Resource. In addition, electronic books are grouped under the E-Book material type.

    Video: includes formats such as DVD, VHS, laserdisc, etc.

    Spoken Recordings: includes formats such as audiobooks.

    Archival Material: Collections of documents, photographs, media, etc. that are the official records of an organization or institution. Archival materials are usually kept in separate collections.

    Marking Records/Making Lists

    I marked some records but I don't see the option to print or email my list.

    When you mark records, you must click the "Save Marked Records" button at the bottom of each page. If you leave a page without clicking this button, your marks will disappear. Once you have saved your marked records, you will see a button in the button bar which allows you to Email/Save/Print your list.

    [back to top]

    How can I print/download/email a list of items from the catalog?

    Do a search and have the search results on your screen.

    1. Place a check mark in the box called "Mark" beside each item you want.
    2. Click the Save Marked Records button at the bottom of the page.
    3. Continue to mark and save records on subsequent pages.
    4. When ready to retrieve your list, click the Email/Save button on the button bar.
    5. The screen that appears will provide options for emailing, exporting or saving the list. You may also display the list on your screen and print it out.
    6. When the operation is complete, click the “Clear Saved Records” button to clear the list.

    The book I want says the status is "Library Use Only". Does that mean I can't borrow it?

    The "Library Use Only" status indicates that the library chooses not to lend. Access to the material may be possible by contacting the owning library to arrange a visit. The material may also be available from another library through interlibrary loan. Your home library‘s Interlibrary Loan office will help you.

    [back to top]


    My instructor wants us to use recently published materials. How can I tell from my results list when each item was published without having to look at each one individually?

    With the search results on the screen, click the “Limit/Sort” button on the button bar across the top of the screen. On the page that appears, click the “Sort results by year” box and click the “Submit” button. Your search results will be redisplayed in date order, newest to oldest. The date will be visible to the right of each item listed.
